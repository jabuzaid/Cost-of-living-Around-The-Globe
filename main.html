About our project


Study, analyze and visualize the cost of living index & others around the world and be able to display several comparisons based on continents, countries and/or cities

It is well known that the gross domestic product (GDP).GDP = private consumption + gross investment + government investment + government spending + (exports – imports). For the gross domestic product, “gross” means that the GDP measures production regardless of the various uses to which the product can be put. often an indicator for international comparisons. The cost of living are periodically calculated in nearly representative baskets of consumer goods.Many factors are complicating a worldwide comparison.For example a typical apartment may not have a standard size nor fittings,even the quality of a branded packet of butter isn't the same in the Third World. Consequently, comparisons like this have to to be accepted with caution. For this project, we wanted to consider few other factors such as: The price of the cup of coffee, The minimum wage, Cost of housing (rent), Food/grocery Accessibility, Gas price, Internet Accessibility.


We ended up using the cost of living index and the local purchasing power index.

Methodology



Data gathering and processing

Our data came mainly from Kaggle
We wanted to compare the changes in Living'costs over the past three years (2016-2017-2018). Kaggle was an amazing and accurate tool to do so.Once we downloaded the datas, we realized that there were few irregularities.In fact we had to create a new column named "country".We then used an Excel method to properly seperate the states and the countries (only for United States) in order to match the rest of the data.Once done, we were able to properly use the datas. After this first step, we quickly realized that we were facing another challenge which was generating the coordinates for all 500 cities listed in our database.We were ready to spend few days googling every single city and manually entering the matching coordinates.But Allen helpt us creating a loop that simply looped through our database and gave us the correspondng coordinates in our notebook.










Data Visualisation

Once we obtained a clean dataset, we went on Mapbox to get an API key that was then stored in a Config.js in order to generate the upcoming maps. We then created an HTML container in which the maps could be renderred.Then we specified different parameters such as the map container element,the map type to use,the cercle radius, the zoom level at which to display the map and the geographic coordinates of the point on which to center the map. We generated few Marker based on our cleaned database.The maps is based on a rent and local purchase index (which makes it easier to have an accurate measuremnt scale).



What is the Cost of Living Index? To calculate each city's Price Index value, we start by assigning a value of 100 to a central reference city. Once the reference point has been established, the Price Index value of every other city in the database is calculated by comparing their cost of living to the cost of living in Prague.
